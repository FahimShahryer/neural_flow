# What is a neuron?

A **neuron** is the smallest unit of a neural network. It takes some numbers in, squashes them through a simple piece of math, and sends one number out. That's it.

Before we talk about layers, backpropagation, or any of the words that show up in textbooks, you need to feel what one neuron does.

## The math, in one line

A single neuron computes:

$$
y = f(w_1 x_1 + w_2 x_2 + \ldots + w_n x_n + b)
$$

Every input $x_i$ gets multiplied by a **weight** $w_i$. We add a **bias** $b$. Then we pass the whole thing through an **activation function** $f$. The result $y$ is the neuron's output.

That's the entire secret. Everything else you'll learn in this module is built from this.

## Why weights exist

Imagine we're trying to predict the price of a house from its size, number of bedrooms, and location score. Size matters a lot. Bedrooms matter some. Location matters a lot too — but differently.

The neuron doesn't know any of this yet. Its **weights** are the knobs we'll turn (automatically, during training) until the neuron pays the right amount of attention to each input.
